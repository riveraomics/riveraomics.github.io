Actively Recruiting Undergraduate Students

We are looking for two (2) prospective undergraduate students to contribute to pilot investigations. The current core research agenda focuses on utilizing bioinformatics methods to understand RNA misregulation in cancer and degenerative disease. The research opportunity will provide students with mentoring on methods for analyzing next-generation sequencing data, transcriptomic models, and RNA regulatory pathways. The goal is to translate findings into collaborative experimental validations and draw mechanistic conclusions. Students who make significant contributions to producing peer-reviewed publications will be recommended for follow-up compensated biotech research opportunities. The lab is also offering a research opportunity in science communication, to promote alternative career paths. See the Projects page for more information.

Students interested in pursuing a research opportunity must send a one-page letter describing their background in biology or computational science, career goals and aspirations, and how RNA bioinformatics fits into your agenda. Students may also optionally send their Resume and/or Curriculum Vitae for deeper evaluation and consideration.
