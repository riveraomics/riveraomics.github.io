Actively Recruiting Undergraduate Students

The newly established lab is looking for two (2) prospective undergraduate students to contribute to pilot investigations at the newly established Rivera Omics Group. The lab aims to decode the intricate molecular landscapes that shape cell biology and disease by leveraging bioinformatics methods and computational algorithms to integrate genomics, transcriptomics, proteomics, and metabolomics data. The current core research agenda focuses on utilizing bioinformatics methods to understand misregulation of RNA splicing and degradation across distinct cancer types, with the goal of translating findings to therapeutic strategies through collaborative investigations. The lab is also offering a research opportunity in bioinformatics science market research and communication, to promote alternative career paths for students. See the Projects page for more information.

Students interested in pursuing a research opportunity must send a one-page letter describing their background in molecular biology and/or computer science, career goals and aspirations, and how RNA bioinformatics fits into your agenda. Students may also optionally send their Resume and/or Curriculum Vitae for evaluation and consideration.
