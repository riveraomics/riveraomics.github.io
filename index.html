What do we do

Welcome to the Rivera Omics Group, a newly established academic lab focused on performing cutting-edge omics research and bioinformatics analysis to unravel the secrets of life and the mechanisms driving complex disease. The lab aims to leverage advanced technologies and computational algorithms to integrate genomics, transcriptomics, proteomics, and metabolomics data, and decode the intricate molecular landscapes that shape disease. At the forefront of bioinformatics, our brilliant scientists uncover novel biomarkers, decipher disease mechanisms, and transform vast biological data into meaningful insights. Through interdisciplinary collaborations, we bridge the gap between data-driven findings and experimental validations, uncovering intricate patterns of gene network regulation and discovering hidden relationships in biological systems.

Where we are

We are begining operations at the Universidad Ana G. Mendez, Cupey Campus in San Juan, Puerto Rico.

The group is actively recruiting undergraduate students eager to contribute to early-stage projects. Join us on this exciting journey as we we push the boundaries of knowledge, deriving transformative insights and paving the way for groundbreaking biological discoveries.

See the Team page for more information on how to join.

Navigate the website to explore current projects or meet the PI, and discover how our research is shaping the future of biotechnology. Codebase for published analysis is made available through our Github page, and wetlab procedures can be found in our Protocols page.
